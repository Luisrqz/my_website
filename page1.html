1.4 Describe the risks that might impact digital projects – what causes digital projects to fail? Why?


The two major factors that causes digital projects to fail are Technology error and human error theses two factors are always found when making a digital project, these are the causes for both: Technology Error Bad internet access: Routers are the source of internet and can be connected either by cable or Wi-fi. The cable connection can be disrupted by a faulty cable or a faulty pc or router internet socket. The Wi-fi connection can be disrupted by moving to an area away from the router and losing signal. This results in losing un-saved data due to no connection with an app or website. System crash: A system crash is referred to a computer that stops working, the reasons crashes happen are software or hardware failure. The computer is unusable either because its “frozen” (still powered on, but not responsive) or is turned off and will not operate. A system crash mixed with the human error of not saving any work results in the work being lost.[4] Low memory: this happens when the memory limit of the computer is exceeded, when trying to save data or opening a program, a memory full message alert displays because the virtual and random-access memory is full. Make sure the computer has enough memory to run properly. Human Error Short term priorities taking over: Not spending the required amount of time on the project because of other priorities is very common, not having an organized time table in which you assign what to do throughout the day is probably the main fail factor. The initial scope was poorly defined: Not having a clear idea on how to make the digital project and starting it is a big step to failure. Not taking the effort to create a clear plan and idea results in failure. Trying to do too much work all at once: Not being organized in doing a task and finishing it before starting another one delays your progress as there isn’t an exact priority to accomplish. Not being trained enough: Doing a task without the knowledge necessary. Before starting the project do the proper training and learning.

1.7 Describe legal guidelines and constraints that impact digital projects – How does GDPR impact digital projects?

GDPR stands for The General Protection Regulation (2018) and is a European data protection law, its purpose is to give EU individuals know as data subjects more control over how their personal data is processed a range of new obligations on organisations that process and control the processing of personal data. Their personal data consist on information that can be used to identify a living person such as, names, location data, email address, health records, photos etc. Failing to comply with the GDPR’s requirements will leave organisation open to considerable higher penalties than they face under the 1998 Data Protection Act, with maximum fines up to 20 million euros or 4% of annual global turnover, whichever is greater. The new law promotes greater transparency, accountability and aims to increase public trust by giving individual more control over their data.

There are six data processing principles that organisations must demonstrate compliance with:


  1. Processed lawfully fairly and in a transparent manner.

  2. Collected for specified explicit and legitimate purposes.

  3. Adequate, relevant and limited to what is necessary.

  4. Accurate and where necessary kept up to date.

  5. Retained only for as long as necessary.

  6. Processed in an appropriate manner to maintain security.


There are six lawful bases for processing data:

  1. Contractual obligations

  2. Comply with the data controllers’ legal obligations.

  3. Protect the data subjects’ vital interest

  4. For task in the public interest or exercise of authority by the data controller

  5. For purposes of legitimate interests’ pursuit by the data controller

  6. If the data subject gives their explicit consent [5]

The impact GDPR has on digital projects is positive one as it sets boundaries to protect the privacy and identity of the costumers, specially with online sales being more common, costumers must have the security of using their bank details without a threat of someone stealing them without being prosecuted by the justice system.
